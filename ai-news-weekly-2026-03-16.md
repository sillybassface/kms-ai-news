### Key Takeaways for KMS Technology

**1. Shift from time-and-materials to outcome-based pricing — and build the internal metrics to support it.**
ISG and Everest Group both identify the T&M contract model as becoming commercially obsolete as agentic AI performs work at machine speed. Engineering services ACV is up 36% YTD, but the demand is for transformation outcomes, not billable hours. KMS should pilot value-based pricing models with select clients immediately and develop internal frameworks to measure and communicate AI-driven productivity gains. EPAM's trajectory ($105M AI-native revenue in Q4 alone, targeting $600M for 2026) illustrates the scale available to firms that make this shift early.

**2. Build model-agnostic, vertical-specific AI delivery capabilities as defensible IP.**
The week's news illustrates two parallel dynamics: hyperscalers and tier-1 integrators (Accenture + Mistral, GitHub + Claude/Codex/Google/xAI) are consolidating model relationships, while enterprise buyers are demanding proven, vertical-specific outcomes. KMS's defensible position is as a neutral, engineering-led integrator who delivers measurable results in target verticals (healthcare IT, fintech, insurtech) — not as a reseller of any single AI platform. Genpact's "Services-as-Software" repackaging and the Wonderful AI raise ($150M Series B) both signal that the premium opportunity is in reusable agentic workflow accelerators, not custom builds.

**3. Treat Vietnam AI Law compliance as an immediate operational priority, not a future planning item.**
With Vietnam's AI Law operative since March 1, 2026, and full compliance deadlines beginning March 2027 (September 2027 for finance, healthcare, and education), KMS needs to start classification of all AI systems developed or operated in Vietnam now. High-risk AI — including anything touching HR technology, clinical workflows, or financial services for clients — will require conformity assessments, human oversight mechanisms, and national portal registration. The regulatory sandbox provisions also create potential partnership opportunities with Vietnamese government and startup clients worth evaluating in parallel.

On March 13, the EU Council formally agreed its negotiating position on the "AI Omnibus" package, proposing significant simplification of the EU AI Act. **Vietnam's First AI Law Takes Effect — Risk-Based Framework Now Operative**
Sources: [Baker McKenzie](https://www.bakermckenzie.com/en/insight/publications/2026/02/vietnam-artificial-intelligence-law-foundation-and-outlook) | [IAPP](https://iapp.org/news/a/vietnam-s-first-standalone-ai-law-an-overview-of-key-provisions-future-implications) | [Duane Morris Vietnam Blog](https://blogs.duanemorris.com/vietnam/2026/03/03/vietnam-the-first-law-on-artificial-intelligence-what-you-must-know/)

Vietnam's first standalone AI Law became operative March 1, 2026, making Vietnam one of the first Southeast Asian nations to establish a comprehensive, risk-based AI regulatory framework. Three risk tiers apply: High-Risk (mandatory pre-market conformity assessment, human oversight, national portal registration), Medium-Risk (transparency disclosures, periodic audits), and Low-Risk (voluntary best practices). Oversight is centralized under the Ministry of Science and Technology. Compliance deadlines: 12 months for most sectors (March 1, 2027), 18 months for AI in finance, healthcare, and education (September 1, 2027). Regulatory sandboxes and a National AI Development Fund are also established.

> **Why this matters to KMS:** With all AI systems developed or operated at KMS's Vietnam development centers now subject to classification under this law, KMS must immediately begin inventorying and risk-tiering every AI system in its portfolio — any tool touching healthcare workflows, financial services, or HR for clients falls into the high-risk tier with a September 2027 compliance deadline, requiring conformity assessments and human oversight mechanisms that must be designed into current projects now to avoid costly retrofitting.
