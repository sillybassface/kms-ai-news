### Forecast: What Changes From Here

#### I. The World Economy

The global economy is entering a **two-speed AI transition**. Advanced economies are capturing disproportionate AI productivity gains, while emerging economies face disruption to their labor-arbitrage-based growth models.

**Near-term (2026–2027):**
- Global GDP growth holds at ~3.3%, buoyed by AI capital expenditure. The $189B record month of startup funding in February 2026 — 90% AI-related — is pouring capital into infrastructure, agents, and vertical applications.
- **Labor markets are bifurcating.** 55,911 tech workers laid off in Q1 2026 alone. Yet AI-specialized roles (ML engineering, AI safety, agent orchestration) are growing faster than traditional software roles. The net effect is wage compression for commodity skills and wage inflation for scarce AI capabilities.
- **India's IT outsourcing model is under acute stress.** The Nifty IT index fell 24% from its peak. Jefferies cut Indian IT price targets by up to 33%. TCS, Infosys, and Wipro collectively added only 160 net employees in 90 days. The labor-arbitrage model that powered $250B+ in annual Indian IT revenue is being repriced in real time.
- **Risk of AI investment bubble correction.** The IMF and multiple analysts flag that current AI spending growth (~60% YoY in compute infrastructure) is outpacing measurable enterprise productivity gains. If ROI evidence doesn't catch up to capital deployment by late 2026, a correction in AI-adjacent valuations is plausible.

**Medium-term (2027–2030):**
- McKinsey's $13–22T GDP uplift projection assumes AI adoption reaches 50%+ of knowledge-work tasks by 2030. The path to that number runs through exactly the kind of enterprise AI integration and vertical customization that IT services companies deliver.
- The shift from time-and-materials to outcome-based contracts will accelerate. ISG and Everest Group both identify this as structurally inevitable — engineering services ACV is up 36% YTD, but demand is for transformation outcomes, not billable hours.
- Countries with proactive AI regulatory frameworks (EU AI Act, Vietnam AI Law) will attract compliance-sensitive enterprise investment. Countries without clear frameworks (UK copyright still unresolved) will see enterprise AI projects delayed.

#### II. Software Engineering

Software engineering is undergoing its most significant structural transformation since the shift to cloud-native development.

**What's changing now:**
- **AI agents are replacing the junior developer workflow.** 55% of developers regularly use autonomous agents. Claude Code, Codex, and Open-SWE can analyze a codebase, plan implementation, write code, run tests, and open PRs — asynchronously. The "out of the loop" model (human sets constraints, agent executes) is displacing the "pair programming" model.
- **Multi-tool, multi-model is the norm.** 70% of developers use 2–4 AI tools simultaneously. Anthropic's Claude models dominate coding tasks with more mentions than all others combined. Enterprise shops default to GitHub Copilot; smaller teams and startups overwhelmingly favor Claude Code.
- **The role is shifting from implementation to orchestration.** The Anthropic agentic coding report identifies eight structural shifts, with the most consequential being the move from "tactical coding" to "architectural reasoning" — developers increasingly design, prompt, review, and validate rather than write line-by-line code.
- **Entry-level is the highest-risk segment.** Entry-level hiring at top 15 tech firms fell 25%. Atlassian's layoffs targeted content creation, QA, and project management — all areas where AI tools demonstrate the strongest capability. The traditional pathway of learning-by-doing on grunt work is being automated.

**What's coming (2026–2028):**
- **Agent teams, not individual agents.** The industry is moving toward multi-agent orchestration where a lead agent delegates subtasks to specialized agents working in parallel. LangChain's Open-SWE already supports subagent orchestration and Slack/GitHub/Linear integration.
- **Task horizons extending from minutes to days.** Current agents handle minutes-long tasks. The trajectory is toward agents that maintain context over multi-day workflows — managing feature branches, responding to code review feedback, and iterating on test failures autonomously.
- **Testing and validation become the premium skill.** As AI writes more code, the ability to define acceptance criteria, design test frameworks, and validate AI-generated output becomes the irreplaceable human contribution. QA is being disrupted at the execution layer but elevated at the design layer.

**Martin Fowler / InfoQ: Where Do Humans Fit in AI-Assisted Software Development?**
Source: [InfoQ](https://www.infoq.com/news/2026/03/mf-aiassisted-dev/)

Martin Fowler's latest analysis identifies three emerging interaction models for AI-assisted development: "in the loop" (human writes, AI suggests), "out of the loop" (human delegates, AI executes), and "on the loop" (human designs testing frameworks and constraints that shape how agents operate). Fowler argues the "on the loop" model is the most important — it shifts the developer's role to defining acceptance criteria, test strategies, and architectural constraints rather than writing implementation code.

> **Why this matters to KMS:** The "on the loop" model directly maps to the skill profile KMS needs to develop: engineers who can define what "correct" means for healthcare and insurance systems (regulatory compliance, clinical safety, data privacy), not just engineers who can implement features. This reframes KMS's value proposition from "we write code" to "we define and enforce the constraints that make AI-generated code safe for regulated industries."

### United States: White House Sets the Framework

**Trump Administration Unveils National AI Legislative Framework — Federal Preemption of State Laws**
Sources: [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/articles/2026/03/president-donald-j-trump-unveils-national-ai-legislative-framework/) | [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/20/trump-ai-policy-framework.html) | [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/trump-ai-congress-law-framework-legislation-artificial-intelligence-rcna264433) | [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/20/white-house-ai-legislative-framework-congress/)
